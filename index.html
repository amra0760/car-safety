    Car Safety Facts

    Here are some facts and stats about care safety

  • The safest spot in the car is the middle seat in the back

  • Nearly 1.3 million people die in road crashes each year, on average 3,287 deaths a day

  • As many as 50 million people are injured or disabled by road traffic crashes every year

  • Correctly used seat-belts reduce the risk of death in a crash by 61%

  • Simple low-cost engineering measures are saving thousands of lives

    Car Tips & Tricks

    Teens

  • Obey the speed limits

  • Always wear your seat belt

  • Don’t drive like you own the road, drive like you own the car

  • Make sure your car always has gas in it

    • Adults

  • Don’t use your phone or any other electronic device while driving

  • Always wear your seatbelt and drive sober and drug-free

  • Keep 100% of your attention on driving at all times – no multi-tasking

  • Be aware of what other drivers around you are doing, and expect the unexpected

    • Seniors

  • Have your vision checked every 1 to 2 years. An eye doctor can treat many vision problems

  • Keep your windshield, mirrors, and headlights clean. Turn the brightness up on the instrument panel.

  • Avoid high-traffic areas if possible. Drive during the day and avoid rush hour. Find other routes with less traffic.

  • See your doctor if you think that pain or stiffness gets in the way of your driving
